### Revenue Model

[FINDING] The primary monetization channel for Telegram games is token generation events (TGE)
and airdrops — not direct IAP or ad revenue.
[STAT:n] Hamster Kombat distributed $10B across 300M users = ~$33 theoretical value per user.
Revenue drivers in priority order:
1. **Token airdrops / play-to-earn** — primary revenue event (one-time)
2. **Telegram Stars** — in-app currency (real money → Stars → TON), platform takes a cut
3. **In-game advertisements** — rewarded video, banners within the mini app
4. **In-App Purchases (IAP)** — skins, power-ups (secondary)
5. **Affiliate / task redirects** — users redirected to YouTube/App Store earns referral revenue

### Tech Stack

- **Frontend:** React or Vue.js (standard web)
- **Backend:** Node.js, Python, or Go
- **Blockchain:** TON Wallet SDK, TON Connect
- **API:** Telegram Bot API + Mini Apps SDK
- **HTML5 rendering** — runs inside Telegram's WebView, no native app install needed

[LIMITATION] Post-airdrop user retention collapses in most tap-to-earn games. Notcoin and
Hamster Kombat saw >90% DAU decline after TGE. The model is high-upside but one-shot unless
a sustainable gameplay loop exists.

## 2. Discord Activities / Embedded Apps

### Platform Overview

[FINDING] Discord Activities offer a 90/10 developer-favorable revenue split on IAP — the
most generous of any major gaming platform.
[STAT:effect_size] Developer retains 90%; Discord takes 10% (vs Apple/Google 30% cut on mobile).
[STAT:n] 100M+ monthly active Discord users; 15+ games integrated Social SDK as of Dec 2024.

[FINDING] Linked Discord accounts show measurable engagement uplift when gaming inside Discord.
[STAT:effect_size] Median 25% increase in game launch frequency; 16% increase in play session length.
[STAT:n] Data across all games that integrated Discord Social SDK post-Dec 2024 launch.

### Revenue Model

- **IAP (In-App Purchases):** Consumables (gacha tickets, XP potions), subscriptions, skins
- **Free-to-Play mechanics:** Full F2P monetization model enabled natively
- **Developer Funding:** Discord App Pitches program grants up to $30,000 to qualifying developers
- **Discord ARR:** $725M ARR (end 2024), $561M total 2025 revenue — activities contribute indirect value

### Development Requirements

- SDK: Discord Embedded App SDK (open source on GitHub)
- Must be **mobile-compatible** (SDK strictly enforces this as of Dec 2025)
- Runs in WebView inside Discord voice channels
- Access to: user identity, voice presence, friend lists via Social SDK
- Monetization: Native IAP via Discord's payment rails

[LIMITATION] Discord Activities audience is predominantly English-speaking Western users (18–34).
No public data on individual Activity titles' revenue, making ROI estimation speculative.
Platform reach is capped by Discord's total user base (~500-600M registered, ~100M MAU).

## 4. Hybrid-Casual Games Trend

### Definition and Distinction from Hyper-Casual

| Dimension        | Hyper-Casual               | Hybrid-Casual                          |
|-----------------|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Core gameplay   | Instant, one-tap simple    | Simple core + meta-progression layer   |
| Monetization    | ~100% ad revenue           | 40-60% ads + 40-50% IAP                |
| Day-7 retention | ~5–8%                      | ~20%                                   |
| Day-30 retention| ~1% (near zero)            | ~10%                                   |
| Session depth   | 1–3 minutes                | 5–15 minutes                           |
| LTV             | Low ($0.10–$0.50)          | Medium-high ($1–5+)                    |
| Examples        | Flappy Bird, Helix Jump    | Monopoly GO, Royal Match, Coin Master  |

### Market Revenue Data

[FINDING] Hybrid-casual IAP revenue grew 67% YoY in Q1 2025, the fastest-growing segment of mobile gaming.
[STAT:effect_size] Q1 2024 top-10 titles: $52M IAP → Q1 2025 top-10: $87M IAP (+67.3%).
[STAT:n] Q2 2025 top-10: $126M (+100% YoY); H1 2025 top-100: $345M total.
[STAT:ci] Projected full-year 2025 (top 100): ~$690M in IAP revenue.

[FINDING] Monopoly GO and Royal Match together earned $4.2B in 2024, validating the
board-game/puzzle + meta-layer hybrid formula.
[STAT:n] Monopoly GO: $2.2B (2024); Royal Match: $2.0B (2024).
[STAT:effect_size] Combined $4.2B from just 2 titles — top-2 concentration in mobile gaming.

### Meta-Game Systems That Drive Retention

1. **Room/world decoration** — fill environments with unlockable items after level completion
2. **Card collection + gacha** — push players to complete sets; drives both retention and revenue
3. **Collaborative events** — co-op missions that create social buzz and co-play
4. **Umbrella events** — multiple parallel mini-events under one seasonal theme
5. **Plinko / chance mechanics** — high-excitement reward drops (Monopoly GO Peg-E Drop, Royal Match Dragon Nest)
6. **Battle pass** — seasonal progression with premium rewards track

[LIMITATION] Hybrid-casual market is dominated by top 1-2% of titles; small studios face
extreme discoverability challenges. High UA spend required to break through (often $5M-$50M+ for scale).

## 7. Game-as-a-Service for Casual Games

### LiveOps Strategy

[FINDING] Top casual game studios ran nearly 100 live events per month in 2025, with LiveOps
becoming the primary monetization growth engine.
[STAT:n] AppMagic LiveOps Report 2025; data across top 50 casual titles.
[STAT:effect_size] LiveOps intensity increased significantly YoY as studios shifted from
new launches to deepening monetization of existing audiences.

### Event System Best Practices

| Event Type         | Cadence         | Revenue Impact | Mechanism                               |
|--------------------|-----------------|----------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Weekend events     | Fri-Sun (3-day) | Highest        | Leisure time alignment + urgency        |
| Seasonal themes    | Monthly         | High           | FOMO cosmetics, limited collectibles    |
| Card collection    | Ongoing         | Medium-high    | Gacha sets drive long-term engagement   |
| Co-op events       | Bi-weekly       | High           | Social buzz + team accountability       |
| Umbrella events    | Seasonal        | Very high      | Multiple parallel events under one theme|
| Battle pass        | Quarterly       | High           | Predictable recurring revenue           |

[FINDING] Optimal event gap is 12–24 hours between major competitive events to prevent
event fatigue.
[STAT:n] Consensus across AppMagic, AppSamurai, and Adjust 2025 LiveOps reports.

### Social Features That Drive Retention

1. **Gifting (lives, boosters)** — soft social obligation to return
2. **Trading systems** — exchange duplicates with friends (adds social layer)
3. **Co-op team events** — shared goals with team members
4. **Leaderboards (friend-based)** — competition with known contacts > global rankings
5. **In-game chat / stickers** — low-friction social expression

### Reward Bundling (2025 Trend)

[FINDING] The boundary between IAP and ads blurred in 2025 via "Reward Bundling" —
hybrid triggers like "watch ad for 3 days → unlock discounted IAP on day 4."
[STAT:n] First identified by AppMagic LiveOps Report 2025/2026.
[STAT:effect_size] Qualitative uplift reported; no controlled AB test data published.

[LIMITATION] LiveOps requires dedicated live team (2-5 people minimum). Solo devs or
micro-studios cannot sustain 100 events/month. Community-driven event cadence (weekly/monthly)
is a realistic target for small teams.

### Opportunity Matrix

| Channel               | Upside Ceiling | Development Cost | Time-to-Revenue | Risk Level |
|-----------------------|----------------|------------------|-----------------|------------|
| Telegram Mini App (P2E)| Very High ($B) | Low (web stack) | Fast (1-3 months) | High (token-dependent) |

| Steam Indie           | Variable       | Medium-High      | Long (2-4 years) | Very High (discovery) |
| HTML5 Licensing       | Low-Medium ($40K/title) | Low     | Fast (deal-based) | Low        |

### Highest-ROI Path for Small Teams (2026)

1. **Build web-first** (Phaser.js / Godot HTML5 export)
2. **Publish to Poki + CrazyGames** for recurring ad revenue
3. **License HTML5 version** to portals for upfront cash
4. **Port to Telegram Mini App** with TON integration for viral P2E event
5. **Add Discord Activity version** for social multiplayer premium monetization
6. **Iterate with AI tools** to reduce asset/content production costs
